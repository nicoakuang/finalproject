About The Project

This project introduces "Adagio," a system designed to solve the common problem of hearing a song but not knowing its name. Using audio fingerprinting technology, our web-based platform allows users to quickly identify music from various sources. We integrated the AudD and AcrCloud APIs for robust recognition, enhanced with real-time lyrics from Genius and lyrics.ovh. A key innovation is our generative AI-powered search engine, which lets users find songs using natural language, descriptions, or moods.

Our Signature Feature: In-Device Recognition

This is the game-changing tool that sets Adagio apart. Ever heard a song in a Netflix show, a YouTube video, or a game on your computer and desperately wanted to know what it was?

With Adagio, you no longer need to grab your phone and struggle to record the audio. Simply play the media on your device, open our website, and start recognition. Adagio listens directly to your computer's audio output and identifies the song for you‚Äîseamlessly and instantly.

Our Project Journey

From a simple idea to an award-winning project, this was our path. Here's a look at the key stages of development for Adagio.

Phase 1: Concept & Research

Semester 1

We began by selecting our topic and gathering extensive data on Music Information Retrieval (MIR) and existing audio recognition technologies.

Phase 2: System Design

Semester 1-2

The core architecture was designed, and we researched the best technologies to use, settling on a dual-API strategy for maximum recognition accuracy.

Phase 3: Development & Implementation

Semester 2-3

This was the longest phase, involving intense coding for both the front-end interface and the back-end audio processing logic.

Phase 4: Testing & Refinement

Semester 3-4

We rigorously tested the system's accuracy with various music genres and noise levels, fixing bugs and optimizing performance based on the results.

Phase 5: Finalization & Presentation

Semester 4

The final report was written, and we prepared for our public presentation, which culminated in winning an award for our hard work.
